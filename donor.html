Common Questions

What happens during a blood donation?

During a blood donation, a healthcare professional will first check your eligibility through a brief health screening. Then, they'll clean the area on your arm and insert a sterile needle to draw blood. The actual donation takes about 10 minutes, and you'll be monitored throughout the process. Afterward, you'll rest and have refreshments before leaving.

How long does the donation process take?

The entire blood donation process takes about 45-60 minutes from arrival to departure. This includes registration, health screening, the actual donation (which takes about 10 minutes), and a short rest period afterward. We recommend setting aside at least an hour for your visit.

Will donating blood make me weak?

Most people feel fine after donating blood. You might experience mild fatigue or lightheadedness, but these symptoms typically resolve quickly after resting and having a snack. We recommend drinking plenty of fluids, avoiding strenuous activity for 24 hours, and eating iron-rich foods to help your body replenish the donated blood.

How will I know when my blood is needed?

After registering, you'll receive notifications via SMS or email when your blood type is needed in your area. These notifications may be for regular donation drives or emergency situations. You can set your notification preferences in your donor profile.
